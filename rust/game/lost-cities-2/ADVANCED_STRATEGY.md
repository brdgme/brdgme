# Lost Cities - Advanced Strategy

Higher-level strategic considerations.

## Expedition economics

- An expedition needs roughly 5+ numbered cards to break even against the cost (20 in 2-player, 15 in 3-player). Fewer cards means a net loss unless the values are very high.
- Wager cards multiply the final score (including the penalty). One wager doubles, two triples, three quadruples. Only commit wagers when the expedition is already profitable or nearly so.
- The bonus for 8+ cards (2-player) or 7+ cards (3-player) is significant. Building a long expedition is often better than spreading cards thin across many expeditions.

## Opponent awareness

- Track which expeditions opponents are building. Avoid discarding cards that extend their expeditions, especially high-value cards or wagers.
- Taking a card from the discard pile denies it to opponents. If they are building an expedition and the top discard helps them, consider taking it even if it is only marginally useful to you.
- Discarding to an expedition no opponent has started is generally safe. Discarding to one they are building is dangerous.
- In a 3-player game, you have two opponents to track. Discards are riskier because either opponent might take them.

## Tempo and round management

- Early in a round, you have more turns to recover from a bad start. Late in a round (deck below ~15 cards), every play matters more.
- If you are losing, consider taking more risks with wagers. If you are winning, play conservatively to protect your lead.
- The player currently in the lead starts the next round. Winning a round narrowly can still be strategically valuable.

## Hand management

- Holding too many cards in unstarted expeditions clogs your hand. If you cannot commit to an expedition within a few turns, discard those cards.
- Wager cards are most valuable early in an expedition. Drawing a wager late, after you have played numbered cards, means it is dead weight - discard it.
- Prefer playing cards that keep your options open. Playing a 5 before a 3 locks you out of playing 3 and 4 later.

## When not to play

- Sometimes the best move is to discard rather than start a weak expedition. A penalty for a 2-card expedition is worse than discarding and waiting.
- If the deck is nearly empty and your expedition is underwater, do not throw good cards after bad. Cut your losses.
