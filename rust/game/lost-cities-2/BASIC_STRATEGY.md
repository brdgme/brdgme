# Lost Cities - Basic Strategy

Hard rules to avoid obviously bad moves.

## Playing cards

- Never play a card to an expedition where you already have a higher or equal numbered card. Cards must ascend strictly within an expedition.
- Never play a wager (X) card after you have played any numbered card in that expedition. Wagers must come first.
- Do not start an expedition with a single low card (2, 3, or 4) unless you hold at least 2 more cards of that expedition in hand. Starting an expedition costs points, and a lone low card guarantees a loss.
- Do not play a wager card unless you have at least 3 numbered cards for that expedition in hand or already played. Wagers multiply losses too.

## Discarding

- Do not discard a card that an opponent is likely to take. Check their expeditions before discarding.
- Prefer discarding cards from expeditions you have not started and do not plan to start.
- Do not discard a wager card for an expedition an opponent is building with wagers.

## Drawing and taking

- Only take from a discard pile if the card directly helps an expedition you are committed to. Taking reveals your strategy and costs tempo.
- You cannot take the same expedition card you just discarded. Plan your discard-then-take accordingly.
- When the deck is low, be cautious about discarding high-value cards opponents need.

## General

- If you hold cards in an expedition but cannot play them in ascending order, discard the lowest unusable card rather than blocking your hand.
- Do not start more than 3 expeditions in a round unless you have strong cards in each. Every started expedition costs points.
- In a 3-player game, the expedition cost is lower (15 vs 20) and the bonus threshold is 7+ cards instead of 8+. Adjust your commitment thresholds accordingly.
