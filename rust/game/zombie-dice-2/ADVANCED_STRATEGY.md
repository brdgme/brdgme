# Zombie Dice - Advanced Strategy

Higher-level strategic considerations.

## Risk calculation

- The bust probability depends on the dice composition in the cup. With 2 shotguns already taken, rolling a single die from a cup of 3 red dice gives a 50% bust chance. Rolling from 3 green dice gives only ~17%.
- Each brain you bank this turn is at risk. The more brains you have accumulated, the more you stand to lose. The decision to roll should weigh potential gain against potential loss.
- Expected value of rolling: if the cup has G green, Y yellow, R red dice, the probability of a shotgun on one die is (G + 2Y + 3R) / (6(G + Y + R)). Use this to assess risk.

## Game position

- If you are in the lead, play conservatively. Bank early, avoid red dice, and let opponents take risks to catch you.
- If you are behind, you must take more risks. Rolling with 2 shotguns is sometimes correct when you need a big turn to stay in the game.
- In the final round (someone has 13+), the pressure shifts. The leader wants to end the game; trailing players want to extend it.

## Rolloff strategy

- In a tie-breaker rolloff, only tied players take turns. The goal is to have strictly more brains than the other tied players when play wraps back.
- In a rolloff, aggressive play is often correct. You need to outscore your tied opponents, so banking small amounts may not be enough.
- Watch your opponents' rolloff turns. If they bank a large number, you need to match or exceed it.

## Multiplayer dynamics

- In games with many players (5+), turns come around less frequently. This means you have fewer opportunities to score, making each turn more valuable.
- With many players, the 13-brain threshold is reached faster. Adjust your banking threshold downward in large games.
- The cup refills more often in multiplayer games because more dice are drawn per round. This means red dice cycle back into play more frequently.

## The psychology of push-your-luck

- The temptation to roll "just one more time" is the core trap. Set a banking threshold before your turn starts and stick to it.
- A turn with 0 brains is always acceptable if you avoided a bust. Do not chase losses by rolling aggressively after a weak start.
- The best zombie dice players know when to stop. Banking 4-6 brains consistently beats busting while chasing 10+.
