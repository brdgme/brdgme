# Roll Through the Ages - Basic Strategy

Hard rules to avoid obviously bad moves.

## Feeding your city

- Your city must be fed every turn: it eats food equal to your current city/dice count (3 to 7). A shortfall becomes famine disaster points. Never spend your last food on a gamble when you cannot replace it next turn.
- Before growing your city (spending workers on city progress), make sure you can reliably produce the extra food it will demand each turn. A bigger city rolls more dice but also needs more food.
- If food is tight, assign food-or-workers dice to food rather than workers, and consider buying Agriculture or Granaries.

## Rerolling dice

- Skulls are locked in the moment they roll and can never be rerolled. You cannot dodge a skull, so spend your rerolls improving the other dice, not hoping to erase skulls.
- Use your 2 rerolls on dice that do not help your plan. Keeping a mix you are happy with and rerolling the rest is better than rerolling everything blindly.
- The more skulls you accumulate in one turn, the worse the disaster (2 = drought, 3 = pestilence, 4 = invasion, 5+ = revolt). When you already hold several skulls, do not reroll dice just to chase value - you are only adding risk of a worse disaster tier.

## Goods

- You may hold at most 6 goods total unless you own Caravans. Anything over the limit must be discarded at the end of your turn. Do not collect goods you will only be forced to throw away.
- Each good type has its own cap (wood 8, stone 7, pottery 6, cloth 5, spearhead 4). A good past its cap is wasted, so do not stack one type blindly.
- Goods are buying power (their value counts toward development purchases) and, with Commerce, score points. Spend a whole good stack only when it buys you something worth it - naming a good spends the entire stack.

## Buying developments

- A development can only be bought once across the whole game. If a development is central to your plan, buy it before an opponent does - once gone, it is gone for everyone.
- Do not pass the Buy phase with enough buying power (coins + goods value, plus food x6 with Granaries) to afford a development you actually want. Saving coins scores nothing.
- The cheapest developments cost 10. If your total buying power is under 10 you cannot buy anything, so do not hoard small coin amounts expecting to spend them later.

## Monuments

- A monument scores nothing until it is fully built (progress reaches its size). Do not leave a monument one or two workers short if you can finish it - partial progress is worth zero.
- Only the first player to complete a monument scores its higher "first" value. If you can finish a monument before an opponent, doing so is usually worth the workers.

## Disasters

- If you keep taking a particular disaster, buy the development that blocks it: Irrigation stops drought, Medicine stops pestilence, the Wall stops invasion, Religion turns revolt back on opponents.
- A revolt (5+ skulls) wipes all your goods unless you have Religion. Avoid ending a turn holding a large goods stockpile when you are rolling many dice and skulls are likely.
