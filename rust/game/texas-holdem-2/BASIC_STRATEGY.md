# Texas Hold'em - Basic Strategy

Hard rules to avoid obviously bad moves.

## Folding and calling

- Do not fold when you can check for free. If your bet already matches the current bet, checking costs nothing.
- Do not call a bet with a hand that cannot win and no draw to improve on, unless the call is tiny relative to the pot.
- Do not call an all-in-sized bet with only a high-card hand. Calling a big bet needs a real pair or a strong draw.

## Raising

- Do not raise below the minimum raise - the action will be rejected. A raise must be at least the larger of the minimum bet or the largest raise so far.
- Do not raise with a weak hand that you would fold to a re-raise. Either commit with a raise or just call.
- Do not min-raise when you have a very strong hand and want to build the pot; a small raise lets opponents in cheaply.

## Going all in

- Do not go all in with a marginal hand while you still have plenty of money and no pressure. All in commits your whole stack.
- Do not go all in when a call would cost less and you are not trying to force a fold.
- Only go all in below the current bet when calling is impossible (not enough money) or you specifically want to contest with everything you have.

## Betting rounds

- Pre-flop, do not limp (just call the big blind) with premium hole cards like a high pair - raise to thin the field.
- After the flop, do not keep betting into multiple opponents with only a single low pair and no kicker.
- On the river, do not bluff by raising if a call from any opponent beats your actual hand.

## Money management

- Do not chase a flush or straight draw when the bet to call is larger than what you can win on later streets.
- If you are short-stacked, avoid spending your last chips on speculative hands (low suited or gapped cards) that need to hit exactly.
