# Sushizock - Basic Strategy

Hard rules to avoid obviously bad moves.

## Dice management

- Always keep at least one die when re-rolling. You must keep at least one; the game enforces this.
- Do not re-roll dice you need. If you have 2 sushi and want to take a blue tile, keep the sushi and re-roll the rest.
- With 0 remaining re-rolls, you are locked into your current dice. Plan your last roll carefully.

## Taking tiles

- Blue tiles score positively but only up to your red tile count. Taking a blue tile when you have 0 red tiles scores 0 for that blue tile.
- Red tiles are negative but enable blue tiles to score. You need a balance: red tiles unlock blue tile scoring.
- Do not take a red tile unless you have or expect blue tiles to offset it. A -4 red tile with no blue tiles is pure loss.
- When taking a tile, you take the Nth tile where N is your dice count. You cannot choose which tile; plan your dice count to target the tile you want.

## Stealing

- Stealing requires 3+ chopsticks of the matching color. This is a significant dice investment (3 of 5 dice).
- Steal a blue tile from an opponent when you need blue tiles and they have high-value ones. Stealing their top blue tile denies them points.
- Steal a red tile from an opponent to dump negative tiles onto them. This is most effective against opponents with few red tiles.
- With 4+ chopsticks you can steal a specific tile from deeper in their stack. Use this to target their highest blue tile or worst red tile.

## Forced take

- If you cannot take or steal after your final roll, you are forced to take the worst available tile. Avoid this by ensuring you always have at least 1 sushi or 1 bones.
- A roll of all chopsticks with no sushi or bones means a forced take. Re-roll chopsticks if you have no other useful dice.
