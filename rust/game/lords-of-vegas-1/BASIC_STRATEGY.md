# Lords of Vegas - Basic Strategy

Hard rules to avoid obviously bad moves.

## Building

- Only build on lots you own that are not already built. The game rejects builds on unowned, opponent-owned, or already-built lots.
- Never attempt a build you cannot afford. Check the lot's build cost against your cash before committing; the cost is deducted immediately.
- Do not drain your cash to zero on a single build. You get no income during the building phase, so cash spent now is gone for the rest of the game.
- A tile's die value is fixed by the lot (die 1-6). Building a low-die lot next to an opponent's high-die casino means you will not be the boss - avoid paying to build tiles that cannot win control.

## Boss ties

- The highest die in a casino is the boss. If your build joins a casino and ties the highest die, all tied tiles reroll until there is a single winner.
- Do not assume a reroll will favour you. Rerolls are random, so creating a tie you are not favoured to win is a gamble, not a plan.
- Prefer building tiles whose fixed die already beats every die in the casino you are joining, so you become boss without a reroll.

## Cash management

- Cheap lots (die 6, $20 build) cost the most but carry the strongest die; expensive-to-win lots (die 1, $8 build) carry the weakest die. Match the lot to what you can afford and what control you need.
- Build your highest-value (low-cost, high-die) lots first while you have cash, rather than spending early on lots that cannot contest control.

## Ending your turn

- You may build any number of times before ending your turn. Build everything useful you can afford before playing `done`, since passing gives up the chance to act until the turn comes back around.
