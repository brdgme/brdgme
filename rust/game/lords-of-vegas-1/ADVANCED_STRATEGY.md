# Lords of Vegas - Advanced Strategy

Higher-level strategic considerations.

## Casino control

- A casino is a contiguous group of same-colour built lots at the same height. Building a lot of a colour next to an existing casino of that colour joins it, so a single tile can swing control of a whole casino.
- The boss is the highest die in a casino. Aim to hold boss positions in the casinos you invest in, since the boss controls how that casino pays out.
- Your tile's die is fixed by the lot. To take boss reliably, build a lot whose die already exceeds every die in the target casino rather than relying on a tie reroll.

## Reading the table

- The casino table shows how many of each casino's 9 cards are still undealt and how many of its 9 lots are still unbuilt. A casino with few cards left is nearly closed to new owners; one with many unbuilt lots is still contestable.
- Track the `played` cards to infer which specific lots opponents may still draw, and which high-die lots of a colour remain available to you.

## Die economics

- Low-die lots (die 1-2) are cheap to build ($6-$8) but weak in boss contests; high-die lots (die 5-6) are expensive ($15-$20) but dominate ties. Spend high-die tiles where control matters and cheap tiles where you just need a footprint.
- Because cash is finite and there is no income during the building phase, treat each build as an irreversible commitment. A tile placed on a losing casino is cash you cannot redirect later.

## Boss tie dynamics

- Tie rerolls cascade: resolving one tie can create another elsewhere in the same casino. A well-timed build can destabilise an opponent's boss position even when you do not win the reroll yourself.
- Conversely, avoid building tiles that hand opponents a cheap reroll chance at your own boss position.

## Positioning

- Build toward connecting your own lots into contiguous casinos you can dominate, rather than scattering isolated tiles across many colours.
- Deny opponents the joining tile when you can: if a lot would let them complete or take over a casino you control, building it yourself (even marginally) can be worth the cost.
