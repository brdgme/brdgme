# No Thanks! - Basic Strategy

Hard rules to avoid obviously bad moves.

## Passing and taking

- Never pass when you have 0 chips. You cannot pass with no chips; you must take.
- Do not pass a low card (3, 4, 5) when you have few chips and no one else is likely to take it. You will end up taking it anyway after wasting chips.
- Take a card when the chips on it exceed the card's likely scoring cost. A card with 5+ chips on it is usually worth taking.
- Do not burn all your chips passing on a single card. Running out of chips forces you to take whatever comes next.

## Run building

- If you hold a card adjacent to the current card (one away), taking it extends a run and the new card may score for free. Prioritize taking cards that complete or extend runs.
- Do not pass a card that would complete a run in your hand unless the chip cost is clearly worse than the scoring benefit.
- Consecutive cards only cost you the lowest value in the run. A run of 8, 9, 10 costs only 8 points, not 27.

## Chip management

- Keep at least 2-3 chips in reserve. Having zero chips means you must take the next card regardless of its value.
- Chips subtract from your final score, so hoarding them is good, but spending them to avoid a high card is better.
- A high singleton card (e.g. 30 with no adjacent cards) costs its full value. Spending 3-4 chips to pass it is usually worth it.
