# No Thanks! - Advanced Strategy

Higher-level strategic considerations.

## Card valuation

- A card's true cost depends on your hand. A 15 is free if you already hold 14 (it extends a run). A 15 costs 15 points if it stands alone.
- Low cards (3-8) are cheap to take even as singletons. High cards (25-35) are expensive and worth spending chips to avoid.
- Cards that bridge two runs are extremely valuable. If you hold 8 and 10, taking a 9 turns two singletons (cost 18) into one run (cost 8).

## Table dynamics

- Watch what other players hold. If the player after you needs the current card, you can pass confidently knowing they will take it.
- If everyone is low on chips, the current card will accumulate many chips before someone is forced to take it. Consider taking early if the card helps your runs.
- In later rounds (few cards remaining), chip conservation matters less because there are fewer opportunities to spend them.

## Timing

- Early in the game, be more willing to spend chips. You have many turns ahead to recoup via chip-laden cards.
- Late in the game (fewer than 5 cards left), take more aggressively. Chips you do not spend are wasted if the game ends before you can use them.
- If you are in the lead (lowest score), play conservatively. If you are behind, take calculated risks with high cards that could complete runs.

## The hidden information

- Nine cards are removed unseen at the start. This means some runs are impossible to complete. Do not count on getting a specific card.
- Track which cards have been taken and by whom. This tells you which runs opponents are building and which cards are still in the deck.
