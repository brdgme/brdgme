# Red7 - Advanced Strategy

Higher-level strategic considerations.

## Rule control

- The player who controls the rule (by discarding) controls the game. Try to keep the rule on a suit where your palette is naturally strong.
- If you have a palette heavy in one color, try to keep the rule on Yellow. If you have many even cards, prefer Green.
- Changing the rule to something obscure (Indigo runs, Violet low cards) can catch opponents off guard if your palette happens to match.

## Palette building

- Build your palette with flexibility in mind. Cards that contribute to multiple rules (e.g., a run of different colors) give you more options when the rule changes.
- High-rank cards are universally useful as tiebreakers. Even if they do not win under the current rule, they help in ties.
- Avoid over-committing to one rule. Your opponent can change it at any time.

## Hand management

- Your hand is your lifeline. Running out means elimination. Balance playing cards (to stay leader) with keeping cards in hand.
- Discarding high-rank cards (rank > palette size) draws a replacement. Use this to cycle through your hand while maintaining options.
- In the early game, prefer playing cards to build a strong palette. In the late game, hoard cards to avoid running out.

## Multi-player dynamics

- With 3-4 players, the leader changes more frequently. Do not assume your lead is safe just because one opponent cannot beat you.
- Watch all opponents' palettes, not just one. The threat can come from any direction.
- In multi-player games, elimination is more common. Playing conservatively (keeping cards) can outlast aggressive players.

## Scoring

- Only the round winner scores, and they score their entire palette. A large palette that wins is worth more than a small one.
- The target score decreases with more players (40 for 2, 35 for 3, 30 for 4). Adjust your risk tolerance accordingly.
- Cards scored carry over between rounds. Building a large palette to win a round gives a permanent scoring advantage.
