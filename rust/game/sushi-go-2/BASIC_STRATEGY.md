# Sushi Go - Basic Strategy

Hard rules to avoid obviously bad moves.

## Card selection

- Do not play a single tempura or sashimi unless you expect to get more. One tempura scores 0; two score 5. One or two sashimi score 0; three score 10.
- Do not play wasabi unless you have a nigiri in hand or expect one soon. Wasabi without a nigiri scores 0.
- Do not play chopsticks unless you have a clear plan to use them next hand. Chopsticks score 0 on their own.
- Pudding scores only at the end of round 3. In rounds 1 and 2, pudding is a low-priority pick unless you are committing to the pudding strategy.

## Maki rolls

- Do not chase maki if you are far behind in maki count. The 6-point bonus goes to the player with the most maki; second place gets only 3.
- If you are leading in maki, consider whether the marginal maki card is worth more than other scoring options.

## Dumplings

- Dumplings scale: 1, 3, 6, 10, 15. The first dumpling is worth 1 point, the fifth is worth 5 marginal points. Commit early or not at all.
- Do not take a single dumpling late in a hand if you have no other dumplings. One dumpling is worth only 1 point.

## 2-player specifics

- Always assign the dummy a card that hurts your opponent least and helps you most. The dummy's cards are visible to you.
- Use chopsticks carefully in 2-player: you must save a card for the dummy, so playing two cards is not always possible.
