# Greed Basic Strategy

## Don't bank zero points
Calling `done` with no turn score wastes your turn entirely. Always score at least one combination before banking, even if it is just a single G for 50.

## Don't roll when you have a comfortable turn score and few dice remaining
With 1 or 2 dice left, your bust probability is high (67% on 1 die, 44% on 2). If you have 300+ banked for the turn, take it.

## Don't ignore the Dollar die
Three Dollars score 600 - the highest triple. When you see Dollars, prioritize setting them aside in groups. A single Dollar scores nothing, but three are very valuable.

## Don't keep rolling on 1 die with a good turn score
A single die only scores on D (100) or G (50). That is a 1-in-3 chance. If your turn score is already 400+, the expected value of rolling does not justify the risk.

## Understand which combinations score before deciding to roll
- Single D = 100, single G = 50 (these are your "safe" dice)
- Three of a kind: $ = 600, G = 500, R = 400, E/e = 300
- Four D = 1000, straight ($GREeD) = 1000, six of a kind = 5000
- R, E, e, and lone $ score nothing by themselves

## Don't be too conservative early
You need 5000 to win. Banking 100-200 per turn will not get you there. Early in the game, push for bigger turns (500+) when the dice are cooperating. Save conservative play for when you are close to 5000.
