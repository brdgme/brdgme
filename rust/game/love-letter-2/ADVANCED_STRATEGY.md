# Love Letter - Advanced Strategy

Higher-level strategic considerations.

## Information and deduction

- Track every discard. With only 16 cards (and several set aside unseen), the discards quickly narrow down what each opponent can hold.
- Use the Priest and Baron to gather information before committing aggressive cards. A confirmed card makes a Guard guess reliable.
- A Baron comparison reveals both cards to the players involved. Even a tie tells you something about the distribution.
- Remember what you learned from a King swap - you saw the hand you traded away, so you know one card that opponent no longer has.

## Card values and round-end

- The round is won by the highest remaining card on a deck-out, with ties broken by the highest total of discarded card numbers. Holding a high card late is valuable.
- The Princess (8) wins any deck-out but eliminates you if discarded. Balance protecting it against the risk of being forced to discard it.
- The Countess (7) and King (6) are strong cards to hold late for a deck-out win.

## Aggressive play

- Use the Prince to force a suspected Princess out of an opponent's hand - if they discard the Princess, they eliminate themselves.
- A King swap is a way to offload a dangerous Princess onto an opponent while taking their card.
- Save the Handmaid for when you hold a high card you want to protect through opponents' turns, or when you are a likely Guard/Baron target.

## Tempo and player count

- In a 2-player game, 4 cards are set aside unseen, so deduction is harder and the Guard is less reliable.
- Eliminating even one opponent in a 3- or 4-player game dramatically shifts the odds, since the round ends as soon as one player remains.
- When you are close to the target score, play to survive the round rather than to eliminate - a single point wins the game.
