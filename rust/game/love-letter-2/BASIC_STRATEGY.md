# Love Letter - Basic Strategy

Hard rules to avoid obviously bad moves.

## The Princess

- Never play the Princess if you have any other card. Discarding it eliminates you from the round immediately.
- If you hold the Princess, plan to play your other card and keep the Princess until you can offload it safely (for example via a King swap or by being forced to discard with the Prince).

## The Countess

- If you hold the Countess together with the King or Prince, you must play the Countess - the game enforces this, so do not waste a turn attempting another card.
- Playing the Countess on its own (without the King or Prince) is safe and reveals nothing about your other card.

## The Guard

- You cannot guess a Guard. Pick a card you have some evidence for - from a Priest peek, a Baron comparison, or what has already been discarded.
- Do not waste a Guard on a guess you cannot justify; a wrong guess does nothing and reveals that you held a Guard.

## The Baron

- The Baron compares the card you keep, not the Baron itself. Do not play the Baron when the card you would keep is low (Guard or Priest) unless you know the opponent's card is lower - you will eliminate yourself.
- Playing the Baron while keeping a high card (Princess, Countess, King) is much safer.

## Targeting

- You cannot target a player who is eliminated or protected by the Handmaid. Check their status before choosing a target.
- If every other player is eliminated or protected, you must target yourself with cards that require a target.
