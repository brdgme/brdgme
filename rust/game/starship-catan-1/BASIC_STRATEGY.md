# Starship Catan - Basic Strategy

Hard rules to avoid obviously bad moves.

## Module selection

- Choose your starting module based on your starting colony's resource. If your colony produces Ore, Command or Logistics pair well. If it produces Trade goods, the Trade module amplifies your income.
- Do not pick a module that does not synergize with your starting position.

## Flight

- You have limited flight actions (base 2, more with Command module). Do not waste them on low-value cards.
- Founding a colony or trading post uses a flight action and a ship. Make sure you have the ship available.
- You cannot found a trading post if you have already traded at that card. Plan your trade-then-found order.
- When the sector pile is empty or you run out of moves/actions, the flight ends. Do not overextend.

## Trading

- During flight, trade stops let you buy or sell resources at the listed price. Check you have enough Astro (money) to buy.
- During TradeAndBuild, you get 2 trades via trading posts. Use them to fill resource gaps for building.
- The Trade module gives extra "take" actions (steal a resource from opponent for 2 Astro). Use these when you need a specific resource urgently.

## Building

- Ships (Colony Ship: 1 Ore + 1 Fuel + 1 Food; Trade Ship: 1 Ore + 1 Fuel + 1 Trade) are essential for expansion. Keep at least one ship available.
- Boosters extend flight distance. Build them when you need to reach deeper into sectors.
- Cannons help defeat pirates. Build them before engaging strong pirates.

## Pirates

- Do not fight a pirate with strength higher than your cannon count + 3 (max dice roll) unless you can afford the loss.
- Losing a fight can destroy a cannon or module. Paying ransom is sometimes cheaper.
- Defeated pirates give medals (for Hero of the People) and victory points.

## General

- The game ends when a player reaches 10 victory points. Track your opponent's VP.
- Colony and trading post cards give victory points. Adventures give victory points. Modules at level 2 give victory points.
- Do not ignore the adventure cards. They are a significant VP source.
