# Alhambra - Basic Strategy

Hard rules to avoid obviously bad moves.

## Taking money

- You may take one card of any value, or multiple cards whose total value is 5 or less.
- Do not take a single card worth 1 or 2 when higher-value cards are available. You need money to buy tiles.
- Take the currency you need most. Check which tiles are available and what currency they cost.

## Buying tiles

- You must pay the exact cost or more. If you overpay, your turn ends immediately (no extra action).
- Paying exact change gives you an extra action. Always prefer exact change when possible.
- Do not buy a tile you cannot place. Check that the tile fits on your grid (adjacent to existing tiles, no wall conflicts, reachable from the Fountain).

## Placing tiles

- Tiles must be placed adjacent to existing tiles on your grid.
- Walls on a tile must not conflict with walls on adjacent tiles (a wall on one tile requires a wall on the touching side of the neighbor).
- All tiles must be reachable from the Fountain by walking through open sides (no walls blocking).
- Do not leave tiles unplaced at the end of your turn if you can place them.

## Reserve management

- During the Action phase, you may place one tile from your reserve onto your grid for free.
- You may swap a reserve tile with a tile already on your grid.
- You may remove a tile from your grid back to your reserve.
- Only one reserve action is allowed per turn. Choose the most impactful one.

## General

- Do not hoard money excessively. Tiles score points; money does not.
- Watch the tile bag count. When it runs low, the game is about to end.
