# Alhambra - Advanced Strategy

Higher-level strategic considerations.

## Scoring awareness

- Scoring happens 3 times during the game (triggered by scoring cards in the deck). Each round scores more points for each tile type.
- Round 1 rewards: 1st place gets a small amount. Round 2: larger rewards for 1st and 2nd. Round 3: largest rewards for 1st, 2nd, and 3rd.
- Focus on tile types that are underrepresented among opponents. If everyone is building Towers, Gardens may be easier to win.
- Walls score every round based on your longest external wall segment. Building a long continuous wall is a reliable scoring strategy.

## Tile placement strategy

- Plan your grid layout to maximize wall scoring. External walls (walls on the perimeter of your Alhambra) score points.
- Keep your grid compact. Gaps and holes are illegal, but an efficient shape minimizes wasted wall segments.
- Place tiles with walls on the outside of your grid to build your external wall length.
- Reserve tiles with useful wall configurations for positions where they extend your longest wall.

## Money management

- Exact change is powerful. Holding cards that sum to common tile costs (3-6) gives you flexibility.
- In the early game, take money aggressively to build a diverse hand. In the late game, spend aggressively to place tiles before the game ends.
- Multiple currencies give you access to more tiles. Do not over-specialize in one currency.

## Market awareness

- Watch which tiles opponents need. If a tile would complete their set for a scoring category, consider buying it even if it is only marginally useful to you.
- The market refills after each turn. A tile you skip may be bought by the next player.
- In 2-player games, Dirk (the AI) accumulates tiles automatically. He competes for scoring categories, so factor his tile counts into your strategy.

## End-game play

- When the tile bag runs low, prioritize buying and placing tiles immediately. Unplaced tiles in your reserve or place area may not get placed.
- In the FinalPlace phase, remaining market tiles are distributed to players with the most of that currency. Stock up on currencies you want to win tiles in.
- Every tile placed before the final scoring counts. Do not waste turns on reserve management when you could be buying tiles.
