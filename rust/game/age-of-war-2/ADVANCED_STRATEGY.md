# Age of War - Advanced Strategy

Higher-level strategic considerations.

## Clan completion priority

- Clan bonuses are the primary scoring engine. Oda (10 for 4 castles), Tokugawa (8 for 3), and Uesugi (8 for 2) offer the best point-per-castle ratios.
- Uesugi is the most efficient clan: only 2 castles for 8 points. Prioritize Kasugayama and Kitanosho.
- If an opponent is close to completing a clan, consider stealing one of their castles to break the bonus. A single steal can deny 8-10 points.

## Dice management

- You start with 7 dice. Each roll costs one die. Plan your attack to minimize rolls: complete lines in an order that leaves useful dice for remaining lines.
- Infantry lines are more flexible than symbol lines because any infantry die contributes. Symbol lines need exact matches.
- Daimyo dice are the rarest useful face (1/6 chance per die). If you need a Daimyo for a steal or a line, the odds of rolling one are low. Commit to Daimyo-dependent plans only when you already hold one.

## Turn tempo

- Conquering a castle ends your turn. A 1-line castle is the fastest way to score and pass play.
- Failed attacks waste your entire turn. Assess your dice carefully before declaring an attack.
- In multiplayer games, turns come around less often. Make each turn count by targeting castles you can actually conquer.

## Opponent interaction

- Track which clans opponents are building. If someone owns 3 of 4 Oda castles, stealing one denies them the 10-point bonus.
- Stealing is most valuable when it breaks a clan bonus. Stealing a single castle from an incomplete clan only denies its individual points.
- In the late game (most castles conquered), stealing becomes the primary way to gain points. Save Daimyo dice for steals.

## Endgame

- The game ends when all 14 castles are conquered. Track how many remain and plan accordingly.
- If you are ahead, prioritize conquering remaining castles to end the game before opponents can steal from you.
- If you are behind, steal high-value castles and break opponents' clan bonuses to close the gap.
