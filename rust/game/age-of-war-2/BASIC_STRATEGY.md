# Age of War - Basic Strategy

Hard rules to avoid obviously bad moves.

## Attacking

- Do not attack a castle you cannot make progress on. If your dice cannot complete even one line, your turn ends immediately with no benefit.
- Do not attack a castle in a clan that is already fully conquered by any player. Those castles are locked.
- Stealing a castle from an opponent adds an extra Daimyo line. Only steal if you have or expect a Daimyo die.
- Prefer attacking castles with fewer lines. A 1-line castle can be conquered in a single successful roll.

## Line completion

- Complete lines you can afford before rolling. Rolling discards a die and shrinks your pool.
- Use your highest-value infantry dice first for infantry lines. The game does this automatically, but understand that a 3-inf die is worth three infantry points.
- Do not complete a symbol line if you need those symbol dice for another line on the same castle. Plan your line order.

## Rolling

- Rolling discards one die and re-rolls the rest. Each roll shrinks your pool. Do not roll if you can complete a line instead.
- If you have 2 dice left and cannot complete any remaining line, your turn will end. Roll only when you have a realistic chance of getting what you need.
- Rolling before declaring an attack is sometimes correct if your initial roll is poor and no castle is attackable.

## Scoring awareness

- Completing a clan (owning all its castles) gives a bonus that replaces individual castle points. The Oda clan bonus (10) exceeds the sum of its castles (7).
- Do not ignore small castles. Odani (1 point) and Inuyama (1 point) are easy to conquer and contribute to clan completion.
- Stealing a castle denies its owner points and gives them to you. Stealing from the leader is doubly valuable.
