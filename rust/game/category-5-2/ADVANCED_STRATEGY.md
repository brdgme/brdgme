# Category 5 Advanced Strategy

- Card counting: track played cards to predict which rows your card will land in and whether a row will hit 5 cards.
- Forcing opponents: play cards that leave rows in states where the next player (by card order) must take a high-bullhead row.
- Timing high vs low cards: play high cards in early turns when rows are short and safe, save low cards for when you need to avoid taking rows.
- Reading opponents: infer likely holdings from what players haven't played - if no one has played below 30, expect low cards to appear soon.
- Strategic sacrifice: take a low-bullhead row deliberately to reset it and avoid being forced to take a worse row later.
- Position play: your seat order relative to row states matters - playing just above a row's last card is safe, but the gap between your card and the row end determines vulnerability to other players' cards resolving before yours.
