# Seven Wonders - Advanced Strategy

Higher-level strategic considerations.

## Drafting strategy

- Card drafting means your choices affect what your neighbors receive. Deny key cards to neighbors who need them.
- Track what you pass. If you pass a strong military card to a neighbor who is already ahead militarily, you are feeding their lead.
- In age 3, guilds appear. These are high-VP cards. Plan your build to maximize guild value.

## Science strategy

- Science scores quadratically: n cards of one field gives n^2 VP. Three different fields (a "set") gives 7 VP per set.
- Committing to science requires 3-4 science cards minimum to be worthwhile. Half-committed science scores poorly.
- Science cards with multiple field choices (wildcards) are extremely valuable. Prioritize them.
- Your neighbors can see your science commitment. They may deny you science cards in the draft.

## Military strategy

- You only need to beat your two immediate neighbors, not the whole table. Calibrate your military to theirs.
- Overshooting military (building 5+ strength when neighbors have 1) wastes resources. Match or slightly exceed.
- Military cards in age 1 are cheap and give early defense. A single barracks or stockade can prevent age 1 defeat.

## Economic strategy

- Coins score 1 VP per 3 at game end. Hoarding coins is a viable but slow strategy.
- Commercial cards (yellow) provide income or discounts. They enable expensive builds later.
- Tavern gives 5 coins immediately. Useful in age 1 when you need coins for age 2 cards.
- Trading posts reduce trade costs from 2 to 1 coin per good. Build them if you plan to trade heavily.

## Wonder priority

- Wonder stages are among the strongest effects in the game. Build them whenever possible.
- Some wonders give free builds (Olympia B stage 2) or extra card plays (Babylon B stage 2). These are tempo advantages.
- The card you discard for a wonder stage is lost. Use a card you would have discarded anyway.

## Age-specific play

- Age 1: Build your resource base. Cheap resource cards and a military card set you up for ages 2-3.
- Age 2: This is where the game is won. Cards are more expensive but more powerful. Commit to your strategy.
- Age 3: Guilds and final scoring. Maximize your VP source (science sets, military dominance, or civic/commercial VP).

## Neighbor awareness

- You interact most with your immediate neighbors (trade, military, card passing).
- If a neighbor is building science, deny them science cards. If they are weak militarily, you can coast on minimal defense.
- Trading posts that discount trade from a specific direction are most valuable when that neighbor has resources you need.
