# Jaipur - Advanced Strategy

Higher-level strategic considerations.

## Timing your sales

- Token piles are finite. Selling early in a good gets you the highest-value tokens. If you wait, your opponent may take them.
- Bonus tokens for selling 3+ are shuffled and hidden. The 5-sale bonus (up to 10 points) is the most valuable. Build toward 5-of-a-kind sales when possible.
- Do not sell a good in small batches (1-2) if you can wait to sell 3+. The bonus token often exceeds the marginal token value.

## Market manipulation

- Taking camels replenishes the market from the deck. If the market has goods your opponent needs, take camels to refresh it.
- Trading goods (take X for Y) lets you upgrade your hand. Swap low-value goods (leather) for high-value ones (diamond, gold).
- Watch what your opponent takes. If they are collecting a good, race them to deplete the token pile first.

## Camel strategy

- The 5-point camel bonus can swing a close round. If you are behind on goods, accumulate camels.
- Camels are also your trading currency. Balance between holding camels for the bonus and spending them for goods.
- Taking all camels is a tempo play: it refreshes the market and denies your opponent camel-trading options.

## Round pacing

- In the first round, establish your strategy. In the second round (if needed), adapt based on what worked.
- If you are winning a round, push to end it quickly (deplete token piles). If losing, slow down and accumulate.
- The game is best-of-3 rounds. Losing one round is not fatal. Do not overcommit to a lost round.

## Opponent awareness

- Track your opponent's hand size and camel count. A full hand means they are about to sell big.
- If your opponent is collecting a specific good, consider taking that good from the market to deny them.
- The market is shared. Every good you take is one your opponent cannot get.

## Token economics

- Precious goods (diamond, gold, silver) have fewer tokens (5 each) but higher values. They deplete fast.
- Common goods (cloth, spice, leather) have more tokens (7-9) but lower values. They are a slower, steadier income.
- A balanced strategy (some precious, some common) is more resilient than going all-in on one good.
