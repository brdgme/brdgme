# Jaipur - Basic Strategy

Hard rules to avoid obviously bad moves.

## Selling

- Do not sell a single camel. Camels cannot be sold; they are only used for trading.
- Precious goods (diamond, gold, silver) require a minimum sale of 2. Do not attempt to sell 1.
- Selling 3+ goods at once earns a bonus token. Always prefer selling in batches of 3+ when you have the goods.
- Token values decrease as the pile is depleted. Early sales of a good are worth more than late sales.

## Taking

- Taking all camels from the market is a free action that replenishes the market from the deck.
- When trading goods (take X for Y), you must give the same number of goods you take. You cannot trade the same type of good.
- Your hand size is limited to 7 non-camel goods. Do not take goods that would exceed this limit.

## Camels

- Camels are for trading, not selling. Accumulate them to swap for valuable goods.
- The player with more camels at round end gets a 5-point bonus. Do not ignore camel accumulation.
- Taking camels replenishes the market, which can deny your opponent a good market.

## Round management

- A round ends when 3 goods have their token piles exhausted, or the deck runs out.
- First to win 2 rounds wins the game. Play aggressively in rounds you are winning.
- Tie-breakers: most bonus tokens, then most good tokens. Collecting bonus tokens matters in close rounds.
