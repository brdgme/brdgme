# Cathedral - Basic Strategy

Hard rules to avoid obviously bad moves.

## Placement rules

- Player 1 must place the Cathedral (piece 1) before any other piece. You cannot skip this.
- You cannot place a piece on occupied cells, off the board, or in territory owned by your opponent.
- You can place pieces in unclaimed territory or your own claimed territory.
- Pieces can be rotated (up, right, down, left). Check all rotations before settling on a placement.

## Captures

- A region is captured when it is fully enclosed by your pieces and contains at most one distinct piece identity (counting the Cathedral as one).
- Do not place a piece that allows your opponent to enclose and capture your pieces. Check whether your placement creates an enclosed region your opponent can claim.
- Captured pieces are returned to their owner's hand and can be replayed. Capturing is powerful but not permanent denial.

## Cathedral placement

- Place the Cathedral near the center of the board to maximize territory division. A corner Cathedral wastes its large footprint.
- The Cathedral is neutral but blocks territory. Place it where it hinders your opponent more than you.
- Once placed, the Cathedral cannot be moved. Choose its location carefully.

## Piece management

- Play your largest pieces first while the board is open. Large pieces become unplaceable as the board fills.
- Keep at least one small piece (size 1 or 2) in reserve for the endgame when only small gaps remain.
- Do not waste small pieces early. They are your lifeline when the board is nearly full.

## Territory

- Claiming territory reduces your opponent's placement options. Every enclosed region you claim is space they cannot use.
- Building along the board edges is efficient: the edge acts as a wall, requiring fewer pieces to enclose a region.
- Do not ignore your opponent's territory claims. If they are enclosing a large region, disrupt it before it is sealed.
