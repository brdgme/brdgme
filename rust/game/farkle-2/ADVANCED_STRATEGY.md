# Farkle Advanced Strategy

## Push-Your-Luck Math

Each roll of N dice has a probability of farkling (no scoring dice). Fewer dice means higher farkle risk:

- 6 dice: ~2.3% farkle chance
- 5 dice: ~7.7%
- 4 dice: ~15.4%
- 3 dice: ~27.8%
- 2 dice: ~44.4%
- 1 die: ~66.7%

Compare the expected value of continuing (average additional points times survival probability) against the certain value of banking your current turn score. When the expected gain from rolling is less than what you stand to lose, bank.

## When to Play Aggressively vs Conservatively

- Behind by 1000+ points: push harder, accept more risk per turn.
- Opponent near 5000: you may only get one or two more turns. Score big or go home.
- Ahead: bank earlier, keep turns to 300-500. Let opponents take the risks.
- Even game in the mid-range: standard risk tolerance, bank around 400-600.

## Hot Dice (All Six Scored)

When all six dice score in a single roll, you re-roll all six. Always take this roll. You have nothing to lose on the re-roll itself since you already banked the points from the scoring dice into your turn score, and a fresh six-dice roll has the lowest farkle probability.

## Target Scores and Risk Tolerance

- Below 1000: you need to get on the board. Take moderate risks to bank your first score.
- 1000-3000: steady accumulation. Bank 400-600 per turn.
- 3000-4500: start watching opponents. If they are close to 5000, push for bigger turns.
- 4500+: one good turn wins. Bank enough to cross 5000 and end the round on your terms.

## Reading the Table

- Track opponents' scores. If someone is at 4500 and you are at 3000, you cannot afford conservative play.
- If you are the closest to 5000, opponents will push harder. Bank reliably and let them farkle.
- In multiplayer games, the player closest to 5000 draws aggression from everyone. Sometimes staying at 4000-4500 until the right moment is better than crossing 5000 early and giving others a target.

## First-Turn Strategy

You must bank at least some points to get on the board. On your first turn, prioritize scoring any available combination and banking it, even if it is only 50-100 points. Getting off zero removes the pressure of needing a minimum threshold later.
