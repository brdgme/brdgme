# For Sale - Advanced Strategy

- Chip management across the full buying phase - budget for high-value cards (25-30) rather than spending evenly
- Selling phase timing - play low cards against weak cheque rounds, save high cards for rounds with large cheques
- Read opponents' likely holdings from the buying phase to predict what they'll play in selling
- Dropping out early (paying half your bid) is often better than winning at full price when the property spread is wide
- Auction position matters: acting last lets you see all bids before committing; acting first means you set the price
- In the selling endgame, calculate exactly which card to play based on remaining cheques and opponents' known hands
